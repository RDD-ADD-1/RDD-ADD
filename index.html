Retrieval-Based Deep Difference Modeling for Audio Deepfake Detection
Abstract
With the rapid advancement of ultra-realistic audio deepfakes, Audio deepfake detection (ADD) has raised serious attention. In recent years, retrieval-based augmentation technology has shown great potential in enhancing ADD by providing additional contextual information. However, existing methods rely on a single database of bonafide audio, which makes it impossible to deeply learn the unbiased difference information between the audio to be detected, the bonafide audio, and the spoof audio. To address this, we propose the Retrieval-based Deep Difference Modeling for ADD, termed RDD-ADD, that integrates the following three novel mechanisms: (1) We proposed a new dual-database retrieval strategy, which jointly utilizes both bonafide and spoof databases to provide richer comparative information for the tested audio. (2) We incorporated Anchor-based similarity fusion and GRU-based contextual fusion mechanisms to perform targeted dynamic fusion of the retrieved features, enhancing their overall representational power. (3) We introduced a graph neural networks based deep difference feature learning strategy, to model the interactions between the tested audio and the retrieved samples and capture the fine-grained differences successfully. Using the above strategies, we can fully learn the in-depth differences between the audio to be tested and the bonafide audio and the spoof audio, so as to achieve robust ADD performance. Experimental results on multiple benchmarking datasets demonstrate that the proposed RDD-ADD outperforms various advanced baselines, providing a more effective solution for retrieval-based ADD. The codes are available at: https://github.com/AI-S2-Lab/RDD-ADD.
